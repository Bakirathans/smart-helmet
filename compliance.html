Mines Safety Regulations (MSHA & OSHA)
Regulatory thresholds and safety protocols for underground mining
Thermal Stress (ACGIH/MSHA)
Underground heat hazards are regulated to prevent heat fatigue and stroke. Dry-bulb limits specify active hydration breaks above 35°C, and work stoppage above 40°C core-equivalent.
CFR 30 § 57.5050
Humidity Index Limits
High relative humidity (> 80%) prevents sweat evaporation, multiplying heat stress index. Continuous exposure under high humidity requires active cooling zones and monitored work-rest cycles.
MSHA Heat Safety Guide
Toxic & Explosive Gases
Combustible gases (Methane) and toxic gases (Carbon Monoxide, LPG) pose severe explosion and asphyxiation risks. MSHA CFR Part 75 mandates alert actions above 200 ppm (combustibles) and instant evacuation if methane exceeds 1.5%.
30 CFR Part 75
